GEMSBOK

The gemsbok (Oryx gazella) is a large antelope species native to arid regions of Southern Africa. Known for its long, straight horns and striking black-and-white facial markings, the gemsbok is well-adapted to survive in desert and semi-desert environments like the Kalahari. Both males and females have horns, which can grow up to 85 cm long, making them excellent for defense.

Gemsbok are herbivores and primarily graze on grasses, herbs, and shrubs. They can survive for long periods without water by extracting moisture from their food and by regulating their body temperature during the heat of the day. Their beige-colored coats reflect sunlight, helping them stay cool in hot climates.

These majestic animals are social and typically found in small herds, although solitary males are also common. Their bold appearance and graceful movements make them one of the most iconic animals in southern African reserves and parks.
